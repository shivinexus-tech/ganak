# Ayyappa Mandala Vratham — Research Draft

**Status:** DRAFT — OWNER REVIEW REQUIRED  
**Research date:** 2026-07-20  
**Scope:** The 41-day discipline, mala, food/conduct, pilgrimage preparation and completion boundaries. No app code or calendar computation has been changed.

## 1. Two timelines Ganak must not confuse

### A. The public Mandala season

The Sabarimala Mandala season begins on the first day of the Malayalam month Vrishchikam and reaches Mandala Pooja at the end of the 41-day period. Ganak already computes this public calendar span. [Kerala Tourism](https://www.keralatourism.org/sabarimala/rituals)

### B. A pilgrim's personal 41-day vratham

The personal vrata begins when the devotee wears the Ayyappa mala and undertakes the discipline. Official Sabarimala guidance says the mala is removed after completing the pilgrimage. Therefore the public “Mandala Pooja” date must not automatically tell every pilgrim to remove the mala: a person's pilgrimage and completion date may be different. [Official Sabarimala portal](https://www.sabarimala.kerala.gov.in/rituals)

Ganak should show both when relevant:

- **Mandala season: day N of 41** — a public temple/calendar span.
- **Your personal vratham: day N of 41** — calculated from the user's mala-wearing/sankalpa date.

If the user has not started a personal vow, the app must not imply that merely viewing the calendar has enrolled them in one.

## 2. Verified core disciplines

Official Sabarimala and Kerala Tourism sources support the following:

- Wear the Ayyappa mala to mark the start of the vrata. It is normally received after prayer from a temple priest or Guru Swami; the official portal also permits wearing it in one's home prayer place. [Official Sabarimala portal](https://www.sabarimala.kerala.gov.in/rituals)
- Observe 41 days of simple, pious and disciplined living. [Official Sabarimala portal](https://www.sabarimala.kerala.gov.in/rituals)
- Maintain celibacy/conjugal abstinence. [Official Sabarimala portal](https://www.sabarimala.kerala.gov.in/rituals), [Kerala Tourism](https://www.keralatourism.org/destination/sabarimala-temple/47/)
- Follow a vegetarian diet and regular prayer. [Kerala Tourism](https://www.keralatourism.org/destination/sabarimala-temple/47/)
- Avoid alcohol and smoking; live without worldly indulgence. [Official Sabarimala portal](https://www.sabarimala.kerala.gov.in/rituals)
- Black clothing is officially recommended as a sign of detachment; Kerala Tourism also records black or blue pilgrim dress. [Official Sabarimala portal](https://www.sabarimala.kerala.gov.in/rituals), [Kerala Tourism](https://www.keralatourism.org/destination/sabarimala-temple/47/)
- The official ritual page records not cutting hair, shaving or trimming nails during the vrata. Ganak should state this as the Sabarimala discipline, not as a generic rule for every Ayyappa prayer outside this vrata. [Official Sabarimala portal](https://www.sabarimala.kerala.gov.in/rituals)

## 3. Guru Swami and pilgrimage-only work

A Guru Swami is described by the official portal as an experienced devotee who has completed 18 Sabarimala pilgrimages. Receiving the mala from a Guru Swami or priest is the normal route, though home wearing is acknowledged. [Official Sabarimala portal](https://www.sabarimala.kerala.gov.in/rituals)

**Irumudi Kettu is not a beginner DIY packing list.** Kettunirakkal—the preparation of the two-compartment Irumudi—is done under Guru Swami guidance. Only pilgrims carrying it may ascend the sacred 18 steps. Ganak should explain this and direct the user to a Guru Swami/temple rather than publish improvised quantities or substitutions. [Official Sabarimala portal](https://www.sabarimala.kerala.gov.in/rituals), [Kerala Tourism](https://www.keralatourism.org/sabarimala/rituals)

Temple rituals such as Neyyabhishekam, Padi Pooja, Kettunirakkal and Aazhi Pooja should be labelled temple/pilgrimage observances. They are not required household actions for every user following the 41-day discipline. [Kerala Tourism — events](https://www.keralatourism.org/sabarimala/events-and-festivals)

## 4. A practical daily structure without inventing lineage rules

The authoritative sources require daily prayer but do not prescribe one universal household timetable or one mandatory text for every region. Ganak may safely organize the verified disciplines like this:

1. **Morning:** bathe/prepare according to family practice, worship Ayyappa and remember the vrata discipline before beginning the day.
2. **Through the day:** vegetarian food; no alcohol or smoking; celibacy; simple conduct and restraint from worldly indulgence.
3. **Prayer:** offer regular Ayyappa prayer, name remembrance or a familiar bhajan received from one's temple/group. Do not invent a compulsory mantra count.
4. **Evening:** pray again and review whether the day's conduct remained within the vow.
5. **Pilgrimage preparation:** coordinate travel, health readiness, mala, Kettunirakkal and Irumudi with the Guru Swami or temple group.

“Swamiye Sharanam Ayyappa” is publicly associated with the pilgrimage, but Ganak need not prescribe a fixed count without a sourced tradition. [Official Sabarimala — Lord Ayyappa](https://sabarimala.kerala.gov.in/index.php/about-lordayyappa)

## 5. Food, fast and health must be separated

The reviewed official sources specify a vegetarian diet and abstinence from vices; they do not support one universal daily nirjala fast or one rigid menu. Ganak should not invent “one meal,” onion/garlic rules or meal times as universal. Those details should come from the user's Guru Swami, temple or family group. [Kerala Tourism](https://www.keralatourism.org/destination/sabarimala-temple/47/)

The Government of Kerala's pilgrimage health guidance explicitly says not to discontinue medicines for the vrata, to carry required medication and a doctor's certificate, prepare physically for the climb, pace the ascent, rest when tired and seek help for concerning symptoms. [Government of Kerala health guidance](https://sabarimala.kerala.gov.in/sites/default/files/press/pr29112019eng.pdf)

This is not a softening of the religious discipline; it is official pilgrimage-safety guidance shown separately.

## 6. Completion and interruption

- **Public calendar:** Mandala Pooja marks the close of the temple's 41-day Mandala period.
- **Personal pilgrim vrata:** the official portal says the mala may be removed after the pilgrimage is completed.
- **Irumudi/18 steps:** completion follows the Guru Swami/temple-led pilgrimage sequence.
- **A personal vow without a Sabarimala trip:** the reviewed official sources do not establish one universal substitute completion or mala-removal rite. This requires temple/Guru Swami guidance.
- **If the discipline is interrupted:** Ganak should not issue a spiritual verdict or casually reset the counter. The user should contact the Guru Swami/temple who gave the vrata and explicitly choose whether and when to restart.

## 9. Proposed English user-facing card

### Ayyappa Mandala Vratham

**What this page is showing**

**[Mandala season: day N of 41 / Your personal vratham: day N of 41].** The public Mandala season and your own 41-day vow are not automatically the same. Your personal vow begins when you knowingly wear the mala and take up the discipline.

**The core discipline**

For 41 days, lead a simple and pious life: worship Lord Ayyappa regularly, follow a vegetarian diet, maintain celibacy, and avoid alcohol, smoking and worldly indulgence. Black is the recommended clothing colour in the Sabarimala tradition. The official practice also avoids hair cutting, shaving and nail trimming during the vrata.

**Beginning the vrata**

Receive and wear the Ayyappa mala after prayer, normally from a temple priest or Guru Swami. The official Sabarimala guidance also permits wearing it in your home prayer place. Record the date only after you intentionally begin; Ganak will not start or reset the vow by itself.

**A simple daily rhythm**

1. Begin the day with Ayyappa worship and remember the discipline you have undertaken.
2. Keep vegetarian food and the additional meal rules given by your Guru Swami or temple.
3. Maintain celibacy, sobriety, truthful and restrained conduct, and a simple way of living.
4. Pray again in the evening; use the Ayyappa prayer, name or bhajan taught in your family or temple group.
5. Prepare for the pilgrimage with your Guru Swami/temple group and attend to physical health as well as ritual preparation.

**Plain-language sankalpa**

“With Lord Ayyappa as my refuge, I begin this 41-day Mandala Vratham and will follow the discipline taught by my Guru Swami or temple with prayer, restraint and sincerity.”

This is a plain-language intention, not a replacement for the initiation or prayer used by your tradition.

**Irumudi and the sacred 18 steps**

Prepare the Irumudi only under Guru Swami guidance. It is the required pilgrimage offering for ascending the sacred 18 steps; Ganak does not provide an improvised do-it-yourself packing ritual.

**Completing the vrata**

Mandala Pooja closes the public 41-day temple season. A pilgrim's mala is removed after completing the pilgrimage according to Guru Swami/temple practice. If you are keeping the vow without travelling to Sabarimala, ask an Ayyappa temple or Guru Swami for the accepted completion; Ganak should not invent one.

If illness or another serious event interrupts the vrata, do not let the app silently reset it. Speak to your Guru Swami or temple and record your chosen next step yourself.

**Health and pilgrimage safety — separate from the religious rule**

Do not stop prescribed medicine for the vrata. Prepare physically for the climb, carry required medicines and medical information, ascend at a safe pace, rest when needed and use the medical centres if unwell. Seek medical advice before undertaking fasting or a strenuous pilgrimage when you have a health condition.

## 10. Proposed Hindi user-facing card

### अय्यप्पा मंडल व्रतम्

**यह पृष्ठ क्या दिखा रहा है**

**[मंडल ऋतु: 41 में से दिन N / आपका व्यक्तिगत व्रत: 41 में से दिन N]।** सार्वजनिक मंडल-काल और आपका अपना 41-दिवसीय व्रत अपने-आप एक नहीं हैं। व्यक्तिगत व्रत तब आरम्भ होता है जब आप सचेत रूप से माला धारण करके नियम ग्रहण करते हैं।

**मुख्य अनुशासन**

41 दिनों तक सरल और पवित्र जीवन रखें: नियमित भगवान अय्यप्पा-पूजन, शाकाहारी आहार, ब्रह्मचर्य, तथा मदिरा, धूम्रपान और सांसारिक विलास से विरति। सबरीमला परंपरा में काला वस्त्र अनुशंसित है। आधिकारिक परंपरा में व्रत के दौरान बाल कटवाना, दाढ़ी बनाना और नाखून काटना भी वर्जित बताया गया है।

**व्रत कैसे आरम्भ करें**

प्रार्थना के बाद अय्यप्पा माला धारण करें—सामान्यतः मंदिर के पुरोहित या गुरु स्वामी से। आधिकारिक सबरीमला मार्गदर्शन घर के पूजा-स्थान में माला धारण करने की अनुमति भी बताता है। तिथि तभी दर्ज करें जब आपने स्वयं व्रत आरम्भ किया हो; गणक अपने-आप व्रत शुरू या रीसेट नहीं करेगा।

**सरल दैनिक क्रम**

1. दिन की शुरुआत अय्यप्पा-पूजन और लिए हुए नियम के स्मरण से करें।
2. शाकाहारी भोजन करें और गुरु स्वामी या मंदिर से मिले अतिरिक्त भोजन-नियमों का पालन करें।
3. ब्रह्मचर्य, नशामुक्ति, सत्य और संयम बनाए रखें तथा सरल जीवन जिएँ।
4. सायंकाल फिर प्रार्थना करें; परिवार या मंदिर-समूह से मिला अय्यप्पा नाम, प्रार्थना या भजन करें।
5. गुरु स्वामी/मंदिर-समूह के साथ यात्रा की धार्मिक और शारीरिक तैयारी करें।

**सरल संकल्प**

“भगवान अय्यप्पा की शरण लेकर मैं यह 41-दिवसीय मंडल व्रतम् आरम्भ करता/करती हूँ और प्रार्थना, संयम तथा श्रद्धा से गुरु स्वामी या मंदिर द्वारा सिखाए नियमों का पालन करूँगा/करूँगी।”

यह सरल भाव-संकल्प है; यह आपकी परंपरा की दीक्षा या प्रार्थना का स्थान नहीं लेता।

**इरुमुडी और पवित्र 18 सीढ़ियाँ**

इरुमुडी केवल गुरु स्वामी के मार्गदर्शन में तैयार करें। पवित्र 18 सीढ़ियाँ चढ़ने के लिए यह आवश्यक यात्रा-अर्पण है; गणक इसकी अनुमानित “स्वयं करें” पैकिंग-विधि नहीं देगा।

**व्रत का समापन**

मंडल पूजा सार्वजनिक 41-दिवसीय मंदिर-काल का समापन है। यात्री की माला यात्रा पूरी होने के बाद गुरु स्वामी/मंदिर की विधि से उतारी जाती है। यदि आप सबरीमला यात्रा के बिना यह व्रत रख रहे हैं तो मान्य समापन के लिए अय्यप्पा मंदिर या गुरु स्वामी से पूछें; गणक कोई विधि नहीं गढ़ेगा।

बीमारी या किसी गंभीर घटना से व्रत बाधित हो तो ऐप इसे चुपचाप रीसेट नहीं करेगा। गुरु स्वामी या मंदिर से बात करके अगला निर्णय स्वयं दर्ज करें।

**स्वास्थ्य और यात्रा-सुरक्षा — धार्मिक नियम से अलग**

व्रत के कारण निर्धारित दवा बंद न करें। चढ़ाई के लिए शरीर को तैयार करें, आवश्यक दवा और चिकित्सकीय जानकारी साथ रखें, सुरक्षित गति से चढ़ें, आवश्यकता पर विश्राम करें और अस्वस्थ होने पर चिकित्सा-केंद्र की सहायता लें। किसी स्वास्थ्य-स्थिति में उपवास या कठिन यात्रा से पहले चिकित्सकीय सलाह लें।

## 12. Integration recommendation — after approval only

- Existing calendar keys: `ayyappaMandalaBegins` and `ayyappaMandalaPuja` should describe the **public season**.
- A future personal tracker requires an explicit user-entered mala date, optional planned pilgrimage date and visible day count; URL/account state only, never browser storage.
- Personal completion must be a user action with a confirmation; it must not be triggered automatically on public day 41.
- Dedicated pages should distinguish `/festival/ayyappa-mandala-begins` and `/festival/ayyappa-mandala-pooja`, while a shared guide explains the span.
