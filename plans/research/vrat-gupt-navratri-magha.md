## What changed in review

**Owner correction after this audit:** KBUF is not a separate tradition, Rajarshi
Nandy is a devotee/teacher whose simple household suggestions can inform research,
and an app is only a delivery tool. These source distinctions remain internal and
are not shown as competing practices on Ganak's public page. The public guide now
uses a single source-neutral household path. The sections below are retained only
to explain the audit history; they are not approved user-facing copy.

The first draft silently combined a Kamakhya-upasaka anushthana with an app-led
Navadurga practice. It also called ordinary lamp-and-mantra worship “yagna”. That
would conflict with Ganak's religious-content policy: the two practices have
different authorities and steps, and **yajna/homa means a fire offering** in the
source used here. They are now labelled separately instead of being presented as
one universal Gupt Navratri method.

The reviewed common path is based on the calendar and Navratri rules that are
shared across the cited sources. It does not publish advanced tantric rites.

## Verified calendar position

- Magha Gupt Navratri begins on **Magha Shukla Pratipada**.
- Drik Panchang's 2026 calendar starts it on **19 January 2026** and lists
  Ghatasthapana followed by the Navadurga calendar sequence.
- A full Navratri fast runs from Pratipada through Navami. **Parana is in
  Dashami after Navami has ended**, following the Nirnaya-Sindhu rule cited by
  Drik Panchang.
- Drik describes Magha Gupt Navratri as mainly observed in northern states. This
  is a regional note, not a restriction on who may worship.

## Complete shared household path

### 1. Before Day 1

- Decide whether the sankalpa is for the complete nine-day fast, one-meal/fruit
  fasting, or daily Devi worship without a food vow. Do not call all three the
  same religious rule.
- Prepare a clean Devi altar, lamp, water, flowers, kumkum, fruit or the family's
  naivedya, and a kalash only if Ghatasthapana is part of the family's practice.
- The product must show the **city-specific Ghatasthapana window**, not merely the
  festival date, before telling a user to install the kalash.

### 2. Day 1 — Ghatasthapana and sankalpa

1. Bathe, wear clean clothes and clean the worship place.
2. During the local Ghatasthapana muhurta, establish the kalash according to the
   family's known method. If the family does not maintain a kalash, begin with a
   clean Devi image and lamp; do not improvise a complex installation from an app.
3. State the sankalpa plainly: the season, the chosen fasting rule, daily worship
   or reading, and the intention to complete the observance after Navami.
4. Offer lamp, water, fragrance/kumkum, flowers and naivedya; recite a familiar
   Devi prayer or begin the chosen Durga Saptashati reading.

### 3. Days 1–9 — daily worship

1. Worship at a consistent time each day with a clean lamp and simple offerings.
2. Continue the mantra, stotra or Durga Saptashati reading named in the sankalpa.
   Ganak should not prescribe a new initiated mantra or a universal chapter split.
3. Keep the chosen food discipline through Navami. Avoid presenting fruit, milk or
   one meal as though it were identical to a complete fast.
4. Drik's calendar names the Navadurga form associated with each day. Ganak may
   show that as calendar context, but this Gupt guide must not become a copy of the
   separate Chaitra/Sharad nine-page Navadurga plan.
5. On Ashtami/Navami, Kumari puja, feeding and homa may be followed where they are
   established family or temple practice; they are not one unlabelled universal
   rule for every household.

### 4. Parana and completion

1. Continue the full fast until Navami has ended.
2. Complete Devi puja, the promised reading/japa and the family's closing offering.
3. Perform parana in Dashami using the **local time Ganak displays**. Begin with
   prasad and the food prescribed by the family's observance.
4. A separately vowed counted anushthana is concluded by the same teacher, temple
   or family rule under which it was undertaken; do not invent a generic udyapan.

## Internal source notes — not user-facing variants

### Kamakhya-upasaka/KBUF anushthana

The reviewed KBUF material describes a specific Maa Kamakhya-centred anushthana
with its own mantra counts and tenth-day completion, including temple/feeding and
an optional properly conducted homa. Show this only as a **named teacher/lineage
method** and only with the complete current instructions from that source.

### Sadhana-app Nav Durga program

The Vedic Sadhana Foundation program is a **guided app practice** with evening
japa and next-morning yagna. Its own public material calls the yagna a fire
offering and says the app program does not require initiation. Ganak must not
replace that yagna with an invented no-fire ritual or make this app program the
default for every Magha Gupt Navratri observer.

### Mahavidya practice

Mahavidya worship is strongly associated with Gupt Navratri in Shakta and tantric
practice, but no stable universal “one Mahavidya per day” order was verified. Do
not publish such an order. Public deity names and context are acceptable; advanced
mantra, nyasa, yantra, bali and homa remain teacher/lineage-guided.
