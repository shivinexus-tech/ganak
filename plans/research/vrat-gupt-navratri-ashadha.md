default.

## Verified calendar position

- Ashadha Gupt Navratri begins on **Ashadha Shukla Pratipada**.
- For New Delhi in 2026, Drik Panchang and Ganak both place Day 1 on
  **15 July 2026**. This directly disproves the stale internal note claiming that
  the observance does not fire in 2026 because of Adhik Maas.
- Drik's 2026 calendar lists Ghatasthapana and the Navadurga calendar sequence and
  also calls this season **Gayatri Navratri**.
- A full Navratri fast continues through Navami; parana is in Dashami after Navami
  ends, under the Nirnaya-Sindhu rule cited by Drik.
- “Varahi Navaratri” is used by some Shakta traditions for this Ashadha period,
  but the reviewed evidence does not support making Varahi worship the universal
  household default.

### English

Ashadha Gupt Navratri is the monsoon Gupt Navratri, beginning on Ashadha Shukla
Pratipada; some calendars also call it Gayatri Navratri. It is commonly observed
through quieter, more personal Devi worship than Chaitra or Sharad Navratri. If
you undertake the complete Navratri fast, continue it through Navami and complete
it in Dashami after Navami ends. A safe household observance is Ghatasthapana, a
daily lamp and Devi puja, a familiar Devi mantra or stotra, Durga Saptashati
recitation, sattvic discipline and charity. Varahi and Mahavidya practices belong
to particular Shakta lineages; advanced mantra, nyasa, yantra, bali or tantric
homa should follow the teacher and tradition from which it was received.

## Complete shared household path

### 1. Before Day 1

- Decide whether the sankalpa is for the complete nine-day fast, one-meal/fruit
  fasting, or daily Devi worship without a food vow. Label the choice accurately.
- Prepare a clean, dry worship space for the monsoon season; lamp, water, flowers,
  kumkum and family naivedya; a kalash only where Ghatasthapana is practised.
- Ganak must calculate the **city-specific Ghatasthapana window** before directing
  the user to establish the kalash.

### 2. Day 1 — Ghatasthapana and sankalpa

1. Bathe, wear clean clothes and prepare the altar.
2. Establish the kalash during the local Ghatasthapana muhurta according to the
   family's known procedure, or begin simple Devi worship without improvising a
   complex kalash rite.
3. State the season, chosen food rule, daily worship/reading and intended Dashami
   completion in the sankalpa.
4. Offer lamp, water, fragrance/kumkum, flowers and naivedya; recite a familiar
   Devi prayer or begin the chosen Durga Saptashati reading.

### 3. Days 1–9 — daily worship

1. Repeat the chosen daily lamp, offerings, prayer and reading consistently.
2. Continue the exact fasting rule taken in the sankalpa through Navami.
3. Drik lists the Navadurga calendar form for each day. This can be shown as
   calendar context, but it must not silently import the Chaitra/Sharad nine-page
   content plan into Gupt Navratri.
4. Ashtami/Navami Kumari puja, feeding or homa should be shown only where they are
   the user's family, temple or named lineage practice.

### 4. Parana and completion

1. Continue a full Navratri fast until Navami has ended.
2. Complete the promised Devi worship, reading or japa.
3. Perform parana in Dashami at the **local time Ganak displays**, beginning with
   prasad and the family's prescribed food.
4. Complete a counted or specialised anushthana under the same family, temple or
   teacher rule with which it began; there is no invented universal udyapan.

## Internal source notes — not user-facing variants

### Gayatri Navratri

Drik uses **Gayatri Navratri** as an alternate name for Ashadha Gupt Navratri.
That name may appear as an alternate calendar label. It is not enough evidence to
publish one universal Gayatri mantra count or homa procedure.

### Varahi Navaratri

Some Shakta lineages identify the Ashadha season with Varahi. Show this as a
lineage/regional variant. Do not prescribe Varahi mantra, yantra, bali or nocturnal
ritual without a qualified lineage source and owner review.

### Sadhana-app Nav Durga program

The Vedic Sadhana Foundation's separate app-led Navdurga method uses evening japa
and next-morning **fire-offering yagna**. The source says its app program does not
require initiation. It remains a named optional program, not Ganak's universal
Ashadha household method; Ganak must not redefine yagna as a no-fire lamp ritual.

### Mahavidya/Kamakhya practice

Mahavidya and Kamakhya practice is important in Gupt Navratri traditions, but no
stable universal day-by-day Mahavidya order was verified. Public context is safe;
advanced practice remains under the relevant teacher or lineage.
