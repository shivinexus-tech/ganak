# Varalakshmi Vratam — Research Draft

**Scope:** Calendar selection plus a South-Indian household guide. No app code or calendar computation has been changed.

## 1. Identity and calendar rule

Varalakshmi is a form of Mahalakshmi worshipped for family well-being, auspiciousness and prosperity. The vrata is especially prominent in Andhra Pradesh, Telangana, Karnataka, Tamil Nadu and Maharashtra and is commonly observed by married women, though Drik notes that it is not inherently restricted to women. [Drik Panchang](https://www.drikpanchang.com/festivals/varalakshmi-vratam/varalakshmi-vratam-date-time.html)

The working calendar rule is the **Friday before Shravana Purnima**, expressed by Drik as the last Friday of Shravana Shukla Paksha. TTD's Sapthagiri publication and two diaspora temples independently describe the Friday before the full moon of Shravana. [Drik Panchang](https://www.drikpanchang.com/festivals/varalakshmi-vratam/varalakshmi-vratam-date-time.html), [TTD Sapthagiri](https://ebooks.tirumala.org/download?id=1286), [Sri Venkateswara Temple, Bridgewater](https://venkateswaratemple.org/Festivals/Varalakshmi.pdf)

The displayed civil date can differ by location because Shravana/Purnima and the applicable Friday are calculated locally. Ganak must calculate from the user's place and explain the result; it must not copy a fixed India date for worldwide users.

## 2. Verified household sequence

TTD's Sapthagiri article provides a detailed living South-Indian sequence:

1. Clean the home and puja place; draw kolam/rangoli.
2. On the vrata morning, bathe and light the lamp.
3. Prepare and decorate a kalasha as Varalakshmi, with local contents varying by region.
4. Place the sacred yellow thread (`saradu`/`dorak`) with the Goddess.
5. Worship Lakshmi with flowers and familiar Lakshmi names/hymns.
6. Offer the family's naivedya, hear the Varalakshmi/Charumathi katha and perform arati.
7. After the puja, wear the blessed thread according to family custom, share tambulam/gifts where practiced, receive prasada and complete the fast.

Source: [TTD Sapthagiri](https://ebooks.tirumala.org/download?id=1286). Drik's full vidhi independently confirms the distinctive dorak thread, naivedya/vayana and formal conclusion steps. [Drik Panchang — full vidhi](https://www.drikpanchang.com/vrats/varalakshmi/puja-vidhi/varalakshmi-puja-vidhi.html)

The full 16- or 32-upachara ritual contains numerous mantras and offerings. Ganak should provide a **simple household path first** and offer the full sourced vidhi as an optional detail or priest-led route, not make every beginner believe all 32 steps are compulsory.

## 3. Fasting and completion

TTD's household description says women fast from morning and eat after completing the puja. This is stronger and clearer than unsourced claims about sunset, moonrise or the next morning. [TTD Sapthagiri](https://ebooks.tirumala.org/download?id=1286)

Ganak should therefore say:

- Keep the morning fast according to the family's food/water rule.
- Perform the Varalakshmi puja in the selected local worship window.
- Receive prasada and complete the fast after the puja, unless the user's own sampradaya gives a stricter rule.

Drik offers four fixed-lagna worship windows and considers an evening window overlapping Pradosha especially suitable, while also allowing any suitable fixed-lagna window. Ganak may show the local choices with a clear “recommended” label rather than imply that only one clock-time is valid. [Drik Panchang](https://www.drikpanchang.com/festivals/varalakshmi-vratam/varalakshmi-vratam-date-time.html)

## 7. Proposed English user-facing card

### Varalakshmi Vratam

**What to do today**

Worship Shri Varalakshmi for the well-being and auspiciousness of the family. Keep your family's morning fast, perform the kalasha puja in a suitable local worship window, hear the vrata story, wear the blessed thread according to family custom, and complete the fast with prasada after the puja.

**Before the puja**

- Clean the worship place and draw the family's kolam or rangoli if practiced.
- Prepare the kalasha, Lakshmi decoration, sacred thread and naivedya according to your regional/family custom.
- Ganak's short list is a preparation aid, not a replacement for an inherited family list.

**Simple household worship**

1. Bathe, light the lamp and state the vrata intention.
2. Invoke Varalakshmi in the prepared kalasha according to your family practice.
3. Offer water, flowers and the family's naivedya; recite familiar Lakshmi names, stotra or prayer.
4. Read or hear the Varalakshmi/Charumathi vrata katha.
5. Perform arati and offer the sacred thread to the Goddess.
6. After the puja, wear the blessed thread and exchange tambulam/gifts if this belongs to your family custom.
7. Receive prasada and complete the fast.

If you do not know the kalasha invocation or full upachara procedure, join a temple or ask a priest/family elder. A simple Lakshmi prayer remains preferable to guessing an initiated or unfamiliar mantra.

**Plain-language sankalpa**

“On this Varalakshmi Vratam, I worship Shri Mahalakshmi and undertake my family's vrata for auspiciousness, right prosperity and the well-being of the whole family.”

This is a plain-language intention, not a prescribed Sanskrit mantra.

**Fast and completion**

Keep the fast from morning according to your family's food and water rule. Complete it with prasada after the Varalakshmi puja unless your sampradaya gives a stricter completion rule.

**Udyapan**

No universal udyapan was verified. A counted or specially undertaken annual vow should be completed under the family, priest or sampradaya that gave its rules.

**Health note — separate from the religious rule**

This note does not change the traditional fast. If strict fasting may be medically unsafe—especially with pregnancy, diabetes, kidney illness, an eating disorder, frailty or medicines—seek medical advice before taking the vow. Do not stop or retime prescribed medicine without professional guidance.

## 8. Proposed Hindi user-facing card

### वरलक्ष्मी व्रतम्

**आज क्या करें**

परिवार के कल्याण और मंगल के लिए श्री वरलक्ष्मी की पूजा करें। परिवार में प्रचलित प्रातःकालीन उपवास रखें, स्थानीय शुभ समय में कलश-पूजा करें, व्रत-कथा सुनें, पारिवारिक रीति से पूजित धागा धारण करें, और पूजा के बाद प्रसाद लेकर व्रत पूरा करें।

**पूजा से पहले**

- पूजा-स्थान स्वच्छ करें और परंपरा हो तो कोलम् या रंगोली बनाएँ।
- अपनी क्षेत्रीय/पारिवारिक रीति के अनुसार कलश, लक्ष्मी-श्रृंगार, पवित्र धागा और नैवेद्य तैयार करें।
- गणक की छोटी सूची केवल तैयारी में सहायता है; वह परिवार से मिली सूची का स्थान नहीं लेती।

**घर पर सरल पूजा**

1. स्नान करके दीप जलाएँ और व्रत का संकल्प करें।
2. पारिवारिक विधि के अनुसार तैयार कलश में वरलक्ष्मी का आवाहन करें।
3. जल, फूल और परिवार में प्रचलित नैवेद्य अर्पित करें; परिचित लक्ष्मी-नाम, स्तोत्र या प्रार्थना करें।
4. वरलक्ष्मी/चारुमती व्रत-कथा पढ़ें या सुनें।
5. आरती करें और पवित्र धागा देवी को अर्पित करें।
6. पूजा के बाद पारिवारिक रीति से पूजित धागा धारण करें और परंपरा हो तो ताम्बूल/उपहार दें।
7. प्रसाद लेकर व्रत पूरा करें।

यदि कलश-आवाहन या पूर्ण उपचार-विधि ज्ञात न हो तो मंदिर में सम्मिलित हों या पुरोहित/परिवार के बड़े से मार्गदर्शन लें। अपरिचित या दीक्षा-आधारित मंत्र का अनुमान लगाने के बजाय सरल लक्ष्मी-प्रार्थना करें।

**सरल संकल्प**

“इस वरलक्ष्मी व्रतम् पर मैं श्री महालक्ष्मी की पूजा करता/करती हूँ और परिवार के मंगल, धर्मसम्मत समृद्धि और सभी सदस्यों के कल्याण के लिए अपने परिवार में प्रचलित व्रत रखता/रखती हूँ।”

यह सरल भाव-संकल्प है, निर्धारित संस्कृत मंत्र नहीं।

**उपवास और समापन**

प्रातः से अपने परिवार के भोजन और जल संबंधी नियम के अनुसार व्रत रखें। वरलक्ष्मी पूजा के बाद प्रसाद लेकर व्रत पूरा करें, जब तक आपके सम्प्रदाय में इससे कठोर समापन-नियम न हो।

**उद्यापन**

एक सार्वभौमिक उद्यापन प्रमाणित नहीं हुआ। निश्चित संख्या या विशेष संकल्प के वार्षिक व्रत को उसी परिवार, पुरोहित या सम्प्रदाय के मार्गदर्शन में पूरा करें जहाँ से उसके नियम मिले हैं।

**स्वास्थ्य-सूचना — धार्मिक नियम से अलग**

यह सूचना पारंपरिक उपवास को नहीं बदलती। गर्भावस्था, मधुमेह, गुर्दे की बीमारी, भोजन-संबंधी विकार, अधिक कमजोरी या नियमित दवा की स्थिति में व्रत लेने से पहले चिकित्सकीय सलाह लें। चिकित्सकीय मार्गदर्शन के बिना निर्धारित दवा बंद न करें और उसका समय न बदलें।
