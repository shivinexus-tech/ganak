## 1. Identity and calendar boundary

Masik Durgashtami is the monthly vrata of Goddess Durga on **Shukla Paksha Ashtami**. Drik Panchang describes worship of Durga and a day-long fast. The Ashwin occurrence during Sharad Navratri is the especially significant Mahashtami, but the monthly observance and the full Navratri Mahashtami guide must not be blended. [Drik Panchang](https://www.drikpanchang.com/vrats/masik-durgashtami-dates.html?lang=en)

It is also distinct from **Kalashtami**, which is associated with Krishna Paksha Ashtami. Ganak must show the paksha and observance name prominently so users do not mistake one for the other.

The date must be calculated for the user's place. Drik's listings show that the civil date depends on local tithi timing and that the panchang day runs from sunrise. [Drik Panchang](https://www.drikpanchang.com/vrats/masik-durgashtami-dates.html?lang=en)

## 2. Verified practice level

- The reliable calendar source supports Durga worship and a day-long fast, but does not publish a complete shastra-derived monthly household paddhati or a universal paran rule. [Drik Panchang](https://www.drikpanchang.com/vrats/masik-durgashtami-dates.html?lang=en)
- Living household-oriented sources commonly describe bathing, cleaning the worship place, lighting a lamp, offering flowers/fruit/water, prayer or familiar Durga recitation, and evening arati. This supports a **simple devotional household path**, not a claim that every item is mandatory. [Sri Mandir](https://www.srimandir.com/articles/vrat-prav-tyohar/masik-durgashtami-2026?lang=en)
- Belur Math explains that people unable to conduct elaborate Durga worship at home commonly visit a local Mother temple after bathing. This supports Ganak explicitly offering “visit a Devi temple” as a complete and respectful option rather than presenting a complicated home ritual as compulsory. [Belur Math](https://belurmath.org/durga-puja-at-belur-math/)
- Formal Durga Puja, Kumari Puja, Chandi ritual, homa, nyasa, initiation-dependent mantra or tantric procedures must not be converted into a do-it-yourself monthly checklist. Belur Math's description itself shows that formal Durga Puja is an extensive consecrated/public rite. [Belur Math](https://belurmath.org/durga-puja-at-belur-math/)

## 3. What Ganak may safely guide at home

1. Bathe and prepare a clean worship place.
2. Place an existing image or murti of Durga in the household shrine; no new consecration is implied.
3. Light a lamp and offer water, flowers and fruit or the family's usual sattvic bhog.
4. Pray in one's own words or recite/listen to a Durga prayer already known and accepted in the family.
5. Visit a Devi temple if that is the family's practice or if formal worship is desired.
6. Complete the chosen day-long fast according to the family's established rule; Ganak must not invent a precise paran time.

This is intentionally a beginner-safe household path. It does not teach bali, homa, nyasa, mudra, initiated mantra, yantra installation or advanced tantric sadhana.

## 4. Fasting variants and religious accuracy

The benchmark source establishes a day-long fast but does not define one universal menu. A household-oriented source records complete/nirjala, fruit-and-milk and one-meal forms, but it is not sufficient authority to make those choices universal law. Ganak should therefore state the traditional day-long vrata and let the observer follow the food rule received from family, guru or temple. [Drik Panchang](https://www.drikpanchang.com/vrats/masik-durgashtami-dates.html?lang=en), [Sri Mandir](https://www.srimandir.com/articles/vrat-prav-tyohar/masik-durgashtami-2026?lang=en)

Ganak must not weaken a user's strict inherited practice. The separate health note may advise medical consultation without rewriting the religious rule.

## 8. Proposed English user-facing card

### Masik Durgashtami

**What to do today**

This is the monthly Shukla Ashtami vrata of Maa Durga. Keep the day-long fast and worship rule followed by your family or temple. At home, a lamp, a simple offering and sincere Durga prayer are enough; formal Chandi or tantric rites require qualified guidance.

**Simple household worship**

1. Bathe and clean the household shrine.
2. Light a lamp before Maa Durga and offer water, flowers and fruit or your family's usual sattvic bhog.
3. State your intention for the day's vrata.
4. Recite or listen to a Durga prayer already known in your family, or pray in your own words.
5. Perform evening arati or visit a Devi temple if that is your practice.

**Plain-language sankalpa**

“On this Masik Durgashtami, I worship Maa Durga and observe the vrata followed in my family, praying for strength, protection and right conduct.”

This is a plain-language intention, not a prescribed Sanskrit mantra.

**Food and fasting**

The traditional observance is a day-long fast. Follow the exact food and water rule received from your family, guru or temple; Ganak does not replace a strict inherited rule with a different one.

**Completing the fast**

Complete the vrata after the day's Durga worship according to your family or temple tradition. A single universal paran clock was not verified for this monthly vrata, so Ganak should not invent one.

**What is not included in this home guide**

Homa, bali, nyasa, mudra, initiated mantra, yantra installation and advanced tantric sadhana are not do-it-yourself instructions. Follow them only within the lineage or temple that authorizes and teaches them.

**Udyapan**

No universal monthly udyapan was verified. For a counted or specially undertaken vow, ask the family priest, guru or temple that gave the observance.

**Health note — separate from the religious rule**

This note does not change the traditional fasting rule. If strict fasting may be medically unsafe—especially with pregnancy, diabetes, kidney illness, an eating disorder, frailty or medicines—seek medical advice before taking the vow. Do not stop or retime prescribed medicine without professional guidance.

## 9. Proposed Hindi user-facing card

### मासिक दुर्गाष्टमी

**आज क्या करें**

यह माँ दुर्गा का मासिक शुक्ल अष्टमी व्रत है। अपने परिवार या मंदिर में प्रचलित दिनभर के उपवास और पूजा-नियम का पालन करें। घर पर दीप, सरल भोग और श्रद्धापूर्वक दुर्गा-प्रार्थना पर्याप्त है; विधिवत चण्डी या तांत्रिक अनुष्ठान योग्य मार्गदर्शन में ही किए जाते हैं।

**घर पर सरल पूजा**

1. स्नान करके घर का पूजा-स्थान स्वच्छ करें।
2. माँ दुर्गा के सामने दीप जलाएँ और जल, फूल तथा फल या परिवार में प्रचलित सात्त्विक भोग अर्पित करें।
3. आज के व्रत का अपना संकल्प स्पष्ट करें।
4. परिवार में परिचित दुर्गा-प्रार्थना का पाठ या श्रवण करें, अथवा अपने शब्दों में प्रार्थना करें।
5. सायंकाल आरती करें या अपनी परंपरा के अनुसार देवी मंदिर जाएँ।

**सरल संकल्प**

“इस मासिक दुर्गाष्टमी पर मैं माँ दुर्गा की पूजा करता/करती हूँ और अपने परिवार में प्रचलित व्रत रखकर शक्ति, रक्षा और सदाचार की प्रार्थना करता/करती हूँ।”

यह सरल भाव-संकल्प है, निर्धारित संस्कृत मंत्र नहीं।

**आहार और उपवास**

पारंपरिक अनुष्ठान दिनभर का व्रत है। भोजन और जल का ठीक वही नियम रखें जो आपके परिवार, गुरु या मंदिर से मिला है; गणक किसी कठोर पारिवारिक नियम को बदलकर दूसरा नियम नहीं बताएगा।

**व्रत कब पूरा करें**

दिन की दुर्गा-पूजा के बाद अपने परिवार या मंदिर की परंपरा के अनुसार व्रत पूरा करें। इस मासिक व्रत के लिए सभी परंपराओं पर लागू एक ही पारण-समय प्रमाणित नहीं हुआ; इसलिए गणक अनुमानित समय नहीं दिखाएगा।

**इस घरेलू मार्गदर्शिका में क्या शामिल नहीं है**

होम, बलि, न्यास, मुद्रा, दीक्षित मंत्र, यंत्र-स्थापना और उन्नत तांत्रिक साधना स्वयं करने की विधियाँ नहीं हैं। इन्हें केवल उस गुरु-परंपरा या मंदिर के मार्गदर्शन में करें जो इनका अधिकार और प्रशिक्षण देता है।

**उद्यापन**

एक सार्वभौमिक मासिक उद्यापन प्रमाणित नहीं हुआ। निश्चित संख्या या विशेष संकल्प के व्रत के लिए उसी परिवार-पुरोहित, गुरु या मंदिर से विधि लें जहाँ से व्रत मिला है।

**स्वास्थ्य-सूचना — धार्मिक नियम से अलग**

यह सूचना पारंपरिक उपवास-नियम को नहीं बदलती। गर्भावस्था, मधुमेह, गुर्दे की बीमारी, भोजन-संबंधी विकार, अधिक कमजोरी या नियमित दवा की स्थिति में व्रत लेने से पहले चिकित्सकीय सलाह लें। चिकित्सकीय मार्गदर्शन के बिना निर्धारित दवा बंद न करें और उसका समय न बदलें।
