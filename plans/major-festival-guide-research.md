# Major festival guide research and editorial boundaries

Status: implemented under `CODEX-P0-MAJOR-FESTIVAL-COVERAGE-ALL`.

## Source method

The guide set uses Drik Panchang for festival identity, tithi/day-part and common
puja structure; Government of India Ministry of Tourism/Culture material for
regional practice and public cultural context; and established temple/community
practice already cited by the existing Ganak guides. Calendar computation remains
owned by Ganak's validated festival engine rather than copied prose dates.

Primary web references used in this pass:

- Drik Panchang festival and puja pages, including Holika Dahan/Holi Puja,
  Akshaya Tritiya and the corresponding festival pages for the reviewed keys:
  `https://www.drikpanchang.com/hindu-festivals/`
- Ministry of Tourism Utsav festival catalogue:
  `https://utsav.gov.in/`
- Ministry of Tourism Incredible India festival catalogue:
  `https://www.incredibleindia.gov.in/en/festivals-and-events`
- Ministry of Culture and International Buddhist Confederation Buddha Purnima
  observances, including the 2026 sacred-relic exposition:
  `https://culture.gov.in/events/ministry-culture-host-sacred-exposition-holy-relics-tathagata-1-14-may-2026-leh-ladakh`
- Incredible India Buddha Purnima overview:
  `https://www.incredibleindia.gov.in/en/festivals-and-events/bihar/buddha-purnima`

## Non-negotiable distinctions encoded in copy and validation

- Holika Dahan is the timed fire-night rite; Rangwali Holi is the next day's
  consent-based colour celebration.
- Akshaya Tritiya never requires buying gold and never promises financial gain.
- Guru Purnima does not legitimise an unverified teacher or remove judgment.
- Dhanteras, Naraka Chaturdashi, Diwali, Govardhan Puja and Bhai Dooj stay
  separate; one generic “Diwali ritual” is not reused across them.
- Gudi Padwa and Ugadi share a tithi but retain their own emblems, foods and
  new-year customs.
- Buddha Purnima is described through Buddhist refuge, ethics, meditation,
  teaching and community practice; Ganak does not convert it into Hindu puja.
- Ganesh immersion, Holika fire, Holi colour, Rath Yatra crowds, Kartika lamps,
  strict fasting and stored Sheetla food all include explicit safety boundaries.
- Existing detailed guides retain their validated fasting/timing/paran rules;
  this pass only completes missing substantive-page sections and steps.

## Excluded claims

No guide guarantees wealth, health, fertility, protection or relationship
outcomes; requires purchases; makes intoxication compulsory; equates worship
with medical treatment; invents Sanskrit mantras; or presents one regional story
as the only pan-Indian origin.
