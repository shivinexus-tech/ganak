# Hartalika Teej puja research and product decision

Date: 2026-07-21  
Task: `CODEX-P0-HARTALIKA-PUJA-01`

## Owner-reported problem

The live festival page reduced the entire puja to one semicolon-separated ingredient
line. It did not tell a devotee what to prepare, what to do first, how the five or
sixteen offerings proceed, or how to close the worship.

## Verified ritual anchors

- Drik Panchang's Hartalika page places the puja in the morning, with Pradosh as an
  alternative when morning worship is not possible. It specifies sand Shiva-Parvati
  forms and narration of the Hartalika legend.
- Drik's Hartalika vidhi summary, explicitly attributed to Vratraj, gives this order:
  early bath and clean dress; sankalpa for Uma-Maheshwara; Ganesha first;
  Shodashopachara of Shiva-Parvati; then Parvati Anga Puja.
- Drik's detailed Shiva Shodashopachara supplies the stable sixteen-offering order
  used for the expanded household path: Dhyana, Sankalpa, Avahana, Asana, Padya,
  Arghya, Achamaniya, Snana, Vastra, Yajnopavita, Gandha, Pushpa, Dhupa, Dipa,
  Naivedya and Tambula.
- The standard Panchopachara core is Gandha, Pushpa, Dhupa, Dipa and Naivedya. Ganak
  places the required Hartalika preparation, Ganesha-first opening and katha/aarti
  closure around those five offerings so it is a complete household procedure.

## Product decision

1. Recommend Panchopachara as the clearest complete household path.
2. Also provide the full sixteen-offering order, collapsed initially so the phone
   page remains navigable.
3. Do not say Shodashopachara is “only for users who know it.” Explain every action.
4. Do not invent mandatory Sanskrit mantras. Users may use their family mantras or
   make each named offering with a plain respectful prayer.
5. Keep deity-specific offering distinctions visible: bilva to Shiva; turmeric,
   kumkum and saubhagya items to Parvati according to family custom.
6. Keep Anga Puja out of the generic DIY sequence because its mantras and exact
   body-part order require the user's established paddhati. This does not remove it
   from the sourced record; it avoids presenting an improvised rite as universal.
7. Keep the owner's religious-policy decision intact: Hartalika remains described
   as traditionally nirjala, with the separate health note not redefining the rule.
