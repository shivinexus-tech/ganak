# Ganak Web GTM v1

**Status:** Owner-requested strategy draft  
**Scope:** Web-first launch; free product; no ads; Android/iOS later  
**Purpose:** Define who Ganak should win first, how to recruit and learn from them,
how beta users differ from early adopters and power users, and what must be true
before public growth begins.

This plan does **not** authorize a public campaign, analytics installation, religious
endorsement claim, monetization change or launch date. Those remain owner decisions.

---

## 1. Executive decision

Ganak should not launch to "everyone interested in astrology." That audience is too
broad, mixes incompatible needs and would push the product toward generic horoscope
content.

The launch wedge is:

> **The Household Panchang Keeper** — a digitally comfortable Hindu adult, usually
> 28–50, who is the person their family asks about vrat and festival dates, local
> timings, fasting or puja guidance, and suitable days for family activities.

They already solve this job through some combination of Google, Drik Panchang,
calendar apps, temple messages, family elders and WhatsApp forwards. They will try a
new product when it reduces cross-checking and gives a clear, locally correct answer
without ads, an account or unexplained technical data.

The highest-propensity first subgroup is:

> **Diaspora Household Panchang Keepers** in the US, Canada, UK, Australia and Gulf
> countries who need city-specific dates and timings, often read in English, and want
> enough explanation to observe at home without losing the Indian context.

This is a launch **wedge**, not a permanent exclusion. India remains the largest
long-term user base. The diaspora subgroup is attractive for beta because its pain is
sharper, users are easy to reach through community networks, and timezone/local-date
failures become visible early.

### Ganak's first public promise

> **Know the date. Know the observance. Know what to do.**

Supporting explanation:

> Ganak gives Hindu households a locally calculated Panchang, fasting and festival
> guidance, and auspicious timings in clear English or Hindi—free, ad-free and without
> requiring an account.

Do not publicly promise "more complete than every Panchang" or "more accurate than
Drik" until independent production comparisons support those claims.

## 2. Why this segment is likely to adopt

The Household Panchang Keeper has all six characteristics of a strong early market:

1. **A recurring job:** tithi, vrat, festival and muhurat questions arise weekly,
   monthly and seasonally.
2. **A meaningful cost of error:** a wrong local date, paran time or festival day
   creates family confusion and damages trust.
3. **Visible current friction:** the user often checks multiple sources or asks a
   family/temple contact to reconcile conflicting answers.
4. **An immediate value test:** within one visit, Ganak can answer a real question for
   the user's city. The user does not have to imagine future value.
5. **Low switching cost:** Ganak is free and requires no login.
6. **Natural distribution:** the answer is commonly shared with a spouse, parent,
   family group, temple group or community organiser.

Market evidence supports the problem's scale without pretending every Hindu is a
target user:

- Google Play shows multiple Panchang/calendar apps with **1M+ downloads**, including
  Drik Panchang and several Hindi calendar products. This validates an existing
  behaviour category; Ganak does not need to teach people what a Panchang is.
- India's Ministry of External Affairs reported **34.36 million overseas Indians as
  of January 2025**, including large populations in the US, Canada, UAE, UK and
  Australia.
- Pew reports that **two-thirds of Indian Americans either identify as Hindu or feel
  close to Hinduism through family or culture**. Among Asian American Hindus, 79% use
  an altar, shrine or religious symbol at home. This supports a substantial
  home-observance use case rather than only temple attendance.
- WhatsApp Communities and announcement groups are explicitly designed around
  organised communities and events, supporting WhatsApp as a recruitment and sharing
  channel. It does not prove that every temple uses WhatsApp, so Ganak must validate
  channel response rather than assume it.

## 3. Segment definition and qualification

### 3.1 Primary ideal early adopter

**Working name:** Household Panchang Keeper  
**Age guide, not a hard filter:** 28–50  
**Religion:** Hindu; Ganak does not target Jain, Buddhist, Christian, Muslim or other
religious calendars  
**Device:** Smartphone-first; Android is especially important in India  
**Language:** English or Hindi today; may speak another regional language at home  
**Geography:** India plus diaspora cities supported by Ganak's location engine  
**Behaviour:** Checks dates, timings or observance guidance at least twice a month  
**Family role:** Makes or influences household observance/planning decisions  
**Current substitutes:** Search, Drik, another Panchang app, printed calendar, temple
messages, WhatsApp forwards, priest/acharya or family elders  
**Core anxiety:** "Is it today or tomorrow here, and what exactly should we do?"

A recruitment candidate qualifies for the core segment when at least **four** of
these are true:

- Checked a tithi, vrat, festival or muhurat in the last 30 days.
- Has compared two sources because dates or instructions differed.
- Is asked by family members for religious-calendar information.
- Observes or helps organise at least two vrats/festivals per month or several major
  festivals per year.
- Needs city/timezone-specific information.
- Has shared a festival date, fasting instruction or puja plan with another person.

### 3.2 Priority subsegments

| Priority | Segment | Sharp need | Role in GTM |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Diaspora household coordinator | Local date/time plus plain-English household guidance | Highest-propensity beta and referral cohort |
| 1 | India-based observant householder | Fast daily answer in Hindi/English; fewer ads and less clutter | Primary scale cohort |
| 1 | Frequent vrat observer | Exact fasting, paran, food and puja guidance | Content-depth validator and repeat user |
| 2 | Temple/community calendar volunteer | Reliable links to send to a group | Distribution partner and correction source |
| 2 | Adult child helping parents | Elder-friendly answer and easy sharing | Usability and family referral cohort |
| 2 | Hindu parent teaching children abroad | Meaning plus practical home observance | Festival-page and story-content cohort |
| 3 | Practising astrologer or serious student | Calculation evidence, advanced Panchang and Prashna | Expert validator and later power-user cohort |

### 3.3 Deliberate non-targets for the first launch

- Casual daily-horoscope entertainment seekers.
- People looking primarily for gemstone, remedy, Vastu or numerology services.
- Users seeking advanced tantric initiation or lineage-restricted practices.
- Non-Hindu religious calendar users.
- Developers seeking the public API; this is a platform segment, not the consumer
  launch wedge.
- Users whose first requirement is saved birth charts or paid consultations.

Not targeting them in the GTM does not require removing every existing feature. It
means acquisition pages, launch messages and onboarding should not be designed around
them.

## 4. Beta users, early adopters and power users are different

| Group | Definition | How they enter | What Ganak owes them | Success signal |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Usability participant | A recruited person completing observed tasks | Direct invitation | A short, respectful session; no expectation of continued use | We learn where navigation or wording fails |
| Beta user | Invited to test before broad promotion and report problems | Screened recruitment | Clear beta status, direct support and visible response to feedback | Completes real tasks and reports useful evidence |
| Early adopter | Chooses Ganak for a real need during the first public phase | Search, referral or community link | Reliable core journey and an easy feedback path | Returns without being chased and shares/uses again |
| Power user | Demonstrates sustained high-value use over time | Emerges from beta/public behaviour | Deeper access, feedback relationship and appropriate tools | Repeated useful actions across several weeks |
| Validator | Has relevant religious, regional or astronomical knowledge | Carefully recruited | Sources, clear scope and no false endorsement claim | Finds a real accuracy/content issue with evidence |
| Advocate | Recommends Ganak to others after receiving value | Emerges organically | Shareable pages and honest product claims | Generates qualified visits or beta recruits |

Do not call every beta tester an early adopter. A friend who opens Ganak once because
the owner asked is a tester, not proof of adoption. Do not define a power user only by
pageviews; repeated confusion can also produce many pageviews.

## 5. Product wedge and launch journeys

### 5.1 Lead with Ganak Panchang, not the whole roadmap

The public entrance should sell three jobs:

1. **What does today mean here?**
   - Local tithi and ending time.
   - Today's vrat/festival.
   - One clear good/avoid summary.
2. **When is the next observance, and how is it followed?**
   - Permanent festival page.
   - Local occurrence.
   - Sourced household guidance and labelled variants.
3. **Which day/time is suitable for my activity?**
   - Structured Muhurat finder.
   - Clear limits when personal chart matching is still required.

Prashna is a secondary product discovery path, not the acquisition headline. Chart,
remedies, gemstones, Vastu and numerology should not dilute the first-launch promise.

### 5.2 Acquisition doorways

Users should not all be forced through the same overloaded Daily page.

| User intent | Best doorway |
|---|---|
| "Today's Panchang in Toronto" | Local Daily page |
| "When is Janmashtami in London?" | Festival page with location selection |
| "How do I observe Hartalika Teej?" | Substantive festival/vidhi page |
| "Housewarming dates in the next 90 days" | Muhurat Finder landing page |
| "What does today's tithi mean?" | Plain-language Panchang explainer |
| Shared family instruction | Permanent page preserving language, place and date context where safe |

### 5.3 First-value standard

The user should receive an understandable answer before being asked to:

- create an account,
- read technical astronomy,
- choose an ayanamsa,
- explore all Ganak features,
- or interpret Sanskrit terminology without a gloss.

Target first useful answer:

- Daily/date check: **under 30 seconds**.
- Find the next festival/vrat: **under 45 seconds**.
- Open and understand a vidhi: **under 60 seconds**.
- Complete a structured Muhurat search: **under 2 minutes**.

These are beta targets to validate, not claims for marketing copy.

## 6. Positioning and messages

### 6.1 Positioning statement

> For Hindu householders who need trustworthy dates, timings and observance guidance,
> Ganak is a local Panchang and worship guide that gives the practical answer before
> the technical data. It is free, ad-free, bilingual and requires no account.

### 6.2 Message hierarchy

1. **Outcome:** Know the date, observance and next step.
2. **Trust:** Calculated for your selected place; traditions and regional variations
   are labelled rather than blended.
3. **Ease:** Plain language first; detailed Panchang remains available.
4. **Respect:** Authentic fasting and worship practices are not silently softened or
   rewritten.
5. **Privacy/value:** No account and no ads. Analytics wording must describe reality
   exactly if anonymous measurement is later enabled.

### 6.3 Messages by segment

| Segment | Lead message |
|---|---|
| India householder | "Today's Panchang and vrat guidance—clear, local and without clutter." |
| Diaspora coordinator | "Indian observances calculated for your city, with practical English guidance." |
| Frequent vrat observer | "Know the correct day, fasting method, puja steps and paran rule." |
| Family helper | "Find the answer and share one clear page with your family." |
| Community admin | "Share a permanent local festival page instead of rewriting the details." |
| Practitioner | "See the plain verdict first, then inspect the calculation and technical evidence." |

## 7. Launch readiness gates

Marketing must not amplify a product that currently confuses users. Public growth
begins only when these gates pass:

### Product and usability

- Daily page has one clear hierarchy and no repeated good/avoid answer.
- Selected-date pages do not call a future/past date "today."
- Location, language and date context remain understandable through each journey.
- Five moderated usability participants complete the three core jobs; high-severity
  navigation failures are fixed and re-tested.
- Phone layouts work at 390px without horizontal overflow or inaccessible controls.

### Accuracy and religious content

- No open severity-1 festival-date, local-time, paran or Muhurat correctness defect in
  the launch journeys.
- Major published vidhis have source traceability and labelled regional/traditional
  variation.
- Pages never invent missing ritual instructions to make content appear complete.
- Lineage-restricted or advanced tantric practice is not presented as generic DIY
  household guidance.

### Operations and trust

- Error monitoring is live and a controlled event has been verified.
- Feedback reaches a monitored owner queue with an expected response cadence.
- Privacy and Terms reflect actual production behaviour.
- Any analytics installation, event list and footer disclosure land together.
- Every public acquisition page has stable metadata, a canonical URL and a shareable
  route.
- A rollback owner and incident-response path are named for launch week.

## 8. Beta programme

### 8.1 Stage A — five observed usability sessions

These are not a marketing beta. They answer whether intended users can operate the
product without coaching.

Recruit:

- 2 India-based household Panchang keepers.
- 1 diaspora household coordinator.
- 2 adults aged 50+ who use smartphones but are not product/technology professionals.

Give each participant the same outcome tasks:

1. "Find whether there is a fast or festival on this date in your city."
2. "Find when the next Ekadashi is and what you would need to do."
3. "Find a suitable housewarming day in the next 90 days."
4. "Change the place and explain whether the date or timing changed."
5. "Share or copy the page you would send to a relative."

Observe without teaching. Record:

- task success,
- time,
- first wrong tap,
- moments of hesitation,
- words the person misunderstands,
- whether they can explain the answer in their own words,
- and whether they know what to do next.

### 8.2 Stage B — private beta cohort of 30

The 30 people are deliberately mixed to expose different failure classes:

| Cohort | Count | Why included |
|---|---:|---|
| India household keepers | 10 | Core scale journey; Hindi/English and mid-range Android |
| Diaspora household keepers | 8 | Timezone, local-date and English-guidance pressure |
| Frequent vrat observers | 5 | Fasting, paran and vidhi depth |
| Adults aged 50+ | 3 | Elder-friendly readability and navigation |
| Temple/community calendar volunteers | 2 | Shareability and group communication |
| Astrologer/Panchang validators | 2 | Calculation terminology and high-risk defect discovery |

Individuals may satisfy more than one characteristic, but the recruitment table must
still meet the minimum exposure (for example, at least three distinct diaspora
timezones and at least four regional/traditional backgrounds).

Recommended diaspora mix:

- US/Canada: at least 3.
- UK/Europe: at least 2.
- Gulf: at least 2.
- Australia/New Zealand or Southeast Asia: at least 1.

Recommended practice mix across the full beta, self-described and never inferred as
formal authority:

- Smarta/general household practice.
- Vaishnava.
- Shaiva.
- Shakta.
- At least two Tamil/South Indian households.
- At least two Bengali/East Indian households.
- At least two North/West Indian regional-practice households.

### 8.3 Beta screener

Ask only what is necessary:

1. In the last month, what religious-calendar question did you look up?
2. What did you use to answer it?
3. Have two sources ever shown you different dates or instructions?
4. Who usually decides or shares festival/fast information in your household?
5. Which city/timezone should Ganak calculate for?
6. Do you prefer English, Hindi or both?
7. Which observances do you follow most often?
8. Would you complete a 20-minute call and send screenshots when something is wrong?
9. What phone/browser do you normally use?

Tradition/region answers are used to test labelled variants, not to rank or judge the
participant's practice.

### 8.4 Invitation copy

> I am building Ganak, a free and ad-free Panchang for Hindu households. It calculates
> dates and timings for your city and explains fasting and festival observances in
> English and Hindi. I am inviting a small group who already checks Panchang or vrat
> dates. This is a beta, so I want you to point out confusing wording, missing
> traditions and wrong dates—not to be polite. Would you use it for two real questions
> over the next two weeks and send me what went wrong?

Do not say "verified by priests" or "approved by astrologers" unless named people have
explicitly approved that public endorsement.

### 8.5 Beta cadence

**Day 0**

- Welcome message with scope, beta status and feedback method.
- One real task, not a feature tour.
- Ask each person to select their actual city and language.

**Days 1–3**

- Watch first-value completion.
- Resolve crashes, blank states, wrong dates and misleading labels first.
- Do not bury critical defects under a large feature request list.

**Day 7**

- Short pulse: "What did you use? What confused you? What did you cross-check?"
- Invite a screenshot or exact URL for every problem.

**Day 14**

- Second task in a different journey.
- Ask whether the user returned without being reminded.

**Day 21**

- 20-minute interviews with at least eight participants: four core householders, two
  diaspora, one elder and one validator.

**Day 28**

- Publish a beta learning report: fixes made, unresolved limitations, segment
  response and whether expansion gates passed.

### 8.6 Beta exit criteria

Targets, not promises:

- At least 80% unaided success on each core task.
- Median Daily answer time under 30 seconds.
- At least 70% can explain the answer and next action correctly.
- Zero unresolved severity-1 date/timing/ritual error in launch journeys.
- At least 12 of 30 return for a second real use without a personal reminder.
- At least 8 of 30 voluntarily share a page or say they would send it to family.
- At least 15 provide one actionable piece of feedback.
- Crash-free production sessions at or above 99.5%, once privacy-compatible
  measurement exists.

Failure to hit a target triggers diagnosis, not metric manipulation. For example,
weak return may mean the observation window lacked a relevant festival, not that the
product has no value.

## 9. Early-adopter programme

### 9.1 Definition

An early adopter is an uncoerced user from the target segment who uses Ganak for a
real household decision during the first public phase and then does at least one of:

- returns on another date,
- opens another observance,
- completes a Muhurat search,
- shares a permanent page,
- or submits useful feedback.

### 9.2 First 100 qualified users

Recruit the first 100 through trust networks, not paid reach:

- 30 from beta referrals and their family groups.
- 20 from temple/cultural/community organisers.
- 20 diaspora householders across at least four timezones.
- 15 frequent vrat observers.
- 10 astrologer/religious-content reviewers.
- 5 owner-network wildcards who still meet the qualification criteria.

This is a sourcing target; people may overlap categories. Track the original referral
source so one large friend group does not masquerade as broad validation.

### 9.3 Offer

Early adopters receive:

- free access to the same core product everyone will receive,
- a direct "Help improve Ganak" route,
- visible release/fix notes,
- optional invitation to the Founding Circle,
- and no manufactured scarcity, lifetime-price promise or religious-authority badge.

The value exchange is: Ganak gives a useful answer; the user gives evidence when it
fails. Access should not be conditional on positive reviews or social promotion.

## 10. Power-user definition and programme

### 10.1 Power users are behaviour-based

Use three power-user types rather than one blended score.

#### A. Devotional power user

Over a rolling 30 days:

- active on at least 6 distinct days,
- opens at least 3 substantive festival/vrat guides,
- returns across at least 3 separate weeks,
- and uses place/date or calendar navigation meaningfully.

#### B. Planning power user

Over a rolling 60 days:

- completes at least 2 Muhurat searches for real activities,
- revisits or shares at least one result,
- and uses both date selection and a ceremony/activity category.

#### C. Expert/validator power user

Over a rolling 60 days:

- inspects technical detail or Prashna evidence repeatedly,
- provides at least one reproducible correction or source-backed content observation,
- and distinguishes a product defect from a legitimate tradition/convention
  difference.

High frequency alone is insufficient. Repeated reloads caused by a broken workflow
must not make someone a power user.

### 10.2 Founding Circle

Create an optional **Ganak Founding Circle** after the private beta:

- 20–40 members maximum in the first 90 days.
- Mix householders, diaspora coordinators, community admins and validators.
- One WhatsApp or Telegram announcement group plus a structured feedback form.
- Monthly 45-minute review, with an agenda and written decisions.
- Members may test features early, vote on clarity and report gaps.
- Religious or calculation claims still require sources and validation; a group vote
  cannot make a rule authentic.
- Public recognition is opt-in only.

Power-user benefits can include early preview links, direct issue acknowledgment and
release-note credit. Do not introduce paid tiers during the learning period.

## 11. Channel strategy

### 11.1 Channel priority

| Priority | Channel | Job | Cost approach | Proof required |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | WhatsApp family/community referrals | Trusted first visits and sharing | Free | Qualified visits, completed core task, shares |
| 1 | Festival/vrat search pages | Capture high-intent questions | Content/SEO effort | Search impressions, useful-answer events, return |
| 1 | Direct beta outreach | Fast qualitative learning | Owner time | Task completion and actionable feedback |
| 2 | Temple/cultural associations | Local trust and diaspora reach | Relationship-led | Referred engaged users, not group size |
| 2 | Small devotional creators | Demonstrate a real answer | No paid endorsement initially | Qualified visits and low correction rate |
| 2 | Astrologer/Panchang reviewers | Accuracy and expert feedback | Relationship-led | Reproducible findings |
| 3 | YouTube explainers | Searchable education and demos | Small production effort | Completion and qualified clicks |
| 3 | Instagram short-form | Awareness around observances | Test only | Saves/shares and useful visits |
| Avoid initially | Broad paid ads | Expensive traffic before retention | ₹0 | Reconsider only after activation/retention |
| Avoid initially | Product Hunt/tech launch | Wrong primary audience | ₹0 | Use only for developer API later |

### 11.2 Content acquisition model

Every content piece must answer a real query and lead to the product answer:

- "When is [festival] in [city/year]?"
- "Is [vrat] today or tomorrow?"
- "What can I eat during [vrat]?"
- "When is paran?"
- "How is [regional variant] different?"
- "What is today's Rahu Kalam in [city]?"
- "Good [activity] dates in the next 90 days."

Do not publish thin pages only to capture search traffic. Each indexed festival page
must be substantive or explicitly limited to verified metadata.

### 11.3 Share loop

The preferred organic loop is:

```text
User receives a local answer
  → shares one permanent page
  → recipient sees the answer without login
  → recipient changes place/date if needed
  → recipient keeps or shares the corrected local page
```

The shared page must not imply that one city's timing applies everywhere.

## 12. Funnel and measurement

### 12.1 Funnel

1. **Qualified arrival:** reaches Ganak with a Panchang/festival/Muhurat need.
2. **First useful answer:** sees a clear relevant answer for a place/date.
3. **Depth:** opens the vidhi, full Panchang or completes a finder.
4. **Return:** comes back for another real need.
5. **Advocacy:** shares a page, refers a user or gives evidence-backed feedback.

### 12.2 North-star metric

**Weekly Useful Answers (WUA):** the number of privacy-safe anonymous sessions in a
week that complete at least one defined answer event:

- Daily answer viewed with valid place/date.
- Festival occurrence/vidhi viewed.
- Muhurat search completed with results or an understandable no-result explanation.
- Prashna answer completed.

Pageviews are not the north star. One user trapped in an overloaded page can generate
many pageviews without receiving value.

### 12.3 Core metric set

| Category | Metric |
|---|---|
| Acquisition | Qualified visits by channel and doorway |
| Activation | % receiving a useful answer; time to first useful answer |
| Comprehension | % who can explain the answer/next step in beta interviews |
| Retention | Return on another day/week and return around a second observance |
| Depth | Festival guide opens, full Panchang opens, completed Muhurat searches |
| Advocacy | Permanent-page shares, referred qualified visits, feedback submissions |
| Quality | Wrong-date/timing reports, blank states, crash-free sessions, stale-context errors |
| Trust | Privacy complaints, correction response time, unresolved source-confidence gaps |

### 12.4 Minimum anonymous event dictionary

Only add after privacy/disclosure approval:

- `daily_answer_viewed`
- `festival_page_viewed`
- `vidhi_opened`
- `muhurat_started`
- `muhurat_completed`
- `muhurat_no_result_explained`
- `prashna_completed`
- `full_panchang_opened`
- `place_changed`
- `language_changed`
- `page_shared`
- `feedback_started`
- `feedback_submitted`
- `calculation_error_visible`

Do not send name, birth details, question text, precise free-text feedback or full
location search text into product analytics. Feedback content belongs in the
explicitly submitted feedback system.

### 12.5 Decision thresholds after 90 days

These are decision aids:

- If Daily activates but festival pages do not retain, improve content navigation and
  local occurrence context before creating more pages.
- If diaspora activation is materially higher, prioritise timezone/share experience
  and English clarity—not paid acquisition.
- If India Hindi users abandon earlier, fix Hindi copy, density and Android
  performance before marketing more widely.
- If Muhurat has high starts but low completion, treat it as product friction, not
  low demand.
- If Prashna attracts repeated use but poor comprehension, keep it secondary until
  the answer/evidence hierarchy is repaired.
- If community-admin referrals show high qualified activation, build better share
  cards and localised group links.

## 13. Feedback operating system

### 13.1 Intake categories

Every report is tagged:

- wrong date/timing,
- wrong or blended tradition,
- missing observance,
- confusing answer,
- navigation failure,
- language/translation,
- accessibility/elder use,
- performance/crash,
- feature request,
- or source/citation question.

### 13.2 Severity

- **S1:** wrong religious date/timing, unsafe/misleading ritual instruction, privacy
  breach, data exposure, or core journey unavailable.
- **S2:** user can complete the task only with a workaround; major language/context
  confusion.
- **S3:** localised confusion or missing secondary information.
- **S4:** enhancement or preference.

### 13.3 Response cadence

- S1: acknowledge immediately when seen; stop promotion of the affected journey;
  investigate before further growth.
- S2: triage within one business day during beta.
- S3/S4: weekly review.
- Publish a plain correction note when a public religious-content or date rule changes
  materially.

The owner receives a weekly one-page report:

- top three user failures,
- S1/S2 status,
- segment and channel response,
- what changed,
- what remains limited,
- and which backlog priorities moved because of evidence.

## 14. Ninety-day rollout

Timing starts only after the launch-readiness gates are green.

### Days -21 to 0: readiness and observed testing

- Fix Daily information hierarchy and stale "today" context.
- Complete five moderated usability sessions.
- Create feedback route and owner queue.
- Approve analytics/privacy bundle or choose manual beta measurement.
- Prepare 10 high-intent, substantive entry pages and permanent links.
- Recruit the 30-person beta but do not broadly promote.

### Days 1–14: private beta

- Onboard 30 participants in two waves of 15.
- Fix S1/S2 defects between waves.
- Run direct interviews.
- Measure task success manually and, only if approved, anonymously in-product.
- Publish beta learning note internally.

### Days 15–30: controlled early access

- Expand to the first 100 qualified users through referrals and communities.
- Test three doorways: Daily, one major festival page and Muhurat Finder.
- Start Founding Circle recruitment from demonstrated contributors.
- Do not buy traffic.

### Days 31–60: soft public launch

- Open public promotion if beta exit criteria pass.
- Publish owner-approved message and share pages.
- Begin weekly high-intent festival/vrat content distribution.
- Partner with 3–5 small community admins or creators, one real use case each.
- Monitor errors/corrections daily around high-intent observances.

### Days 61–90: retention and power users

- Identify the three power-user types from behaviour plus direct feedback.
- Convene the first Founding Circle review.
- Improve the highest-friction returning journey.
- Compare India/diaspora and English/Hindi outcomes without collecting identity.
- Decide whether to expand acquisition, change positioning or hold growth for
  reliability.
- Keep monetization out of the test unless operating cost creates a documented need.

## 16. Risks and countermeasures

| Risk | Countermeasure |
|---|---|
| Launch audience is too broad | Keep acquisition centred on the Household Panchang Keeper |
| UI overload destroys first value | Treat Daily IA as a launch gate |
| A wrong religious date spreads through sharing | S1 stop-promotion rule, local context and correction notes |
| One tradition is presented as universal | Label variants; require sources and relevant validators |
| Friendly testers give praise but do not adopt | Separate testers from uncoerced early adopters |
| Power users distort the mainstream UI | Give technical depth its own progressive layer |
| Diaspora success hides India/Hindi problems | Maintain separate cohort readouts |
| Analytics contradicts privacy promise | Ship instrumentation and disclosure as one reviewed change |
| WhatsApp group becomes unstructured support | Announcement channel plus structured form and weekly triage |
| SEO produces thin devotional content | Substantive-page gate; no invented ritual filler |
| Feature requests derail launch | Prioritise only evidence tied to the three launch jobs |

   scale market.
