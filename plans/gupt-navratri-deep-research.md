# Ganak — Magha and Ashadha Gupt Navratri deep-research brief

Task: `P0-GUPT-NAVRATRI-DEEP-RESEARCH`

Status: **before-live research; no detailed ritual page before owner review**

## Why this needs its own track

Magha and Ashadha Gupt Navratri are frequently described online through a mixture of
general Navratri household practice, modern festival journalism and advanced
Mahavidya/tantric claims. Ganak must not copy the Chaitra/Sharad nine-Navadurga model,
invent a nine-day Mahavidya order, or publish initiated procedures as DIY worship.

## Questions the research must answer

1. What named Sanskrit textual sources establish four Navratris, and what do those
   texts actually say about Magha and Ashadha observance?
2. Which practices are common public household devotion: Ghatasthapana, vrata/food
   rules, diya, simple Devi puja, Durga Saptashati/Devi Mahatmya, Devi Bhagavata,
   charity, Kumari puja or other observances?
3. Is worship of the ten Mahavidyas during Gupt Navratri textually established,
   lineage custom, or a modern popular synthesis? Is there any stable day-by-day
   assignment, especially given ten Mahavidyas across nine nights?
4. Which differences are documented between Magha and Ashadha Gupt Navratri rather
   than merely seasonal/date differences?
5. Which traditions and regions actively observe them, and how do Smarta, Shakta,
   temple and initiated tantric practice differ?
6. Which mantras, nyasa, yantra, homa, bali, purashcharana or Mahavidya procedures
   require diksha/Guru supervision or lineage authority and must not appear as public
   step-by-step instructions?
7. What safe, respectful page can Ganak offer an ordinary householder without
   trivialising a private practice or implying that advanced ritual is universal?

## Required evidence standard

- Start from named primary texts and critical/academic translations where available;
  record chapter/verse or exact section, not “scriptures say.”
- Consult at least two credible living-tradition sources (recognised Shakta
  institutions, temples, acharyas or lineage publications) and label the lineage.
- Use Drik Panchang for date/Ghatasthapana benchmarking, not as sole authority for
  religious practice.
- Treat news/SEO festival articles only as evidence of current popular practice, not
  proof of scriptural or universal rules.
- Record disagreements and absence of evidence. Do not force a single universal
  sequence when sources differ.

## Research deliverables for owner review

1. Separate Magha and Ashadha evidence tables with dates, deciding kala and practices.
