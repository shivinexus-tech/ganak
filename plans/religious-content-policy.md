# Ganak religious-content policy

**Owner direction — 2026-07-20. Applies to every agent and every Hindu observance.**

## Product position

Ganak describes Hindu religious practice faithfully. It does not weaken, replace,
or judge a traditional observance because the practice is strict. Ganak respects
the user's belief, family tradition, sampradaya, region, and chosen level of
observance.

## Religious rule first; safety note separately

1. State the traditional rule clearly. For example, where Hartalika Teej is
   traditionally observed as a nirjala fast, say so plainly.
2. If health context is needed, put it in a visibly separate **Health note**. The
   note must not redefine the vrat, call the traditional rule misleading, or
   present a modified practice as though it were the same religious rule.
3. A health note may advise a user to seek medical and family/religious guidance
   when the traditional observance is unsafe for that person. The choice remains
   with the user.
4. Never measure, rank, or question a user's devotion.

## Variants and disagreements

- Label regional, family, sampradaya, temple, and textual variants explicitly.
- If two established rules differ, show both and explain who follows each. Do not
  silently blend them or let Ganak choose a belief system for the user.
- Keep distinct observances distinct when their purpose, sequence, ending rites,
  or user expectations differ. Chaitra Navratri and Sharad Navratri therefore
  require separate user-facing guides.

## Changes that require owner approval

Agents must ask the owner before they:

- describe a core religious practice as optional, unnecessary, outdated, unsafe,
  or merely symbolic;
- replace a strict fast with fruit, water, or another modified observance;
- remove or soften a traditional prohibition or required ritual;
- merge two distinct festivals, seasons, traditions, or regional practices;
- choose one lineage or regional rule while hiding another established rule; or
- add a product opinion that changes how a user would understand or perform the
  observance.

Agents may correct spelling, broken links, missing card connections, and clearly
separate medical disclaimers without changing the religious instruction. Every
substantive religious-content change remains source-backed and is shown to the
owner during content review.
