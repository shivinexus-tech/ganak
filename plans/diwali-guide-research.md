# Diwali substantive guide — source and product notes

Status: implemented as the first new slice of the major-festival coverage audit.

## Product boundary

- The `/festival/diwali` page covers the main Amavasya night and a simple Hindu
  household Lakshmi path. It does not collapse Dhanteras, Naraka Chaturdashi,
  Govardhan Puja, Bhai Dooj or Kali Puja into one ritual.
- The shared household core is cleaning, lamps, Lakshmi worship, familiar prayer,
  family naivedya, prasad, generosity and gathering. Ganesha worship is common in
  North Indian practice but is described as customary rather than universal.
- A day-long fast is documented, but not imposed as a universal requirement.
  Expensive purchases, new murtis, account-book worship, Kubera worship and a full
  priest-led Shodashopachara remain optional tradition-specific paths.
- Rama's return, Lakshmi on Amavasya and Krishna–Narakasura are labelled by their
  festival-day and regional scope. Bengali Kali Puja, Maharashtrian sequence and
  Tamil Deepavali remain distinct.

## Explicit exclusions

No fabricated Sanskrit sankalpa; no guaranteed-wealth claim; no gambling remedy;
no requirement to buy precious metal; no universal nirjala instruction; no
lineage-specific Kali or tantric procedure; no unsafe instruction to leave an
unattended flame burning overnight.
